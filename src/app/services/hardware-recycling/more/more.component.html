Hardware Recycling explained

With hardware recycling, components and raw materials of waste electronics and hardware products are disassembled and separated. Think of items like memory RAM sticks, motherboards, power supplies, graphics cards and harddrives. These items are often made up from plastic, glass, ferrous metals and non-ferrous metals. Some of these materials can easily be recycled, while others are too complex to recycle.

How we recycle your Hardware with the most of care

So… how does one recycle hardware? Well, it’s a complex process. The hardware is dismantled with care and then sorted according to the type of materials like steel, aluminum or glass. These kinds of materials can be recycled to make vehicle parts or new IT hardware products, whereas other materials that can’t be recycled will be disposed of in an environmentally friendly way.

The importance of Hardware Recycling for your company (and our planet)


By recycling your hardware, you avoid waste being dumped into nature and you reduce the amount of raw materials that are needed to make (new) products. If you don’t reuse the hardware yourself, it can benefit many people and companies that have a small budget.
By choosing to recycle your hardware, you contribute to a circular economy and a more environmentally friendly planet. In the face of climate change, it’s essential to make these kinds of conscious choices that benefit our world.

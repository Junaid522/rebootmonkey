What is a Service Level Agreement?


A Service Level Agreement is a contract between a client and a service provider. The contract covers specific elements of the delivered service. Think of aspects like quality, availability and reliability. The contract identifies who is responsible in case certain criteria aren’t met, and who should compensate potential losses.

Such an agreement is essential for a professional and accurate collaboration between client and service provider. That’s why at Reboot Monkey we strongly recommend making use of an S.L.A.

Remote Hands SLA


Remote hands Service Level Agreements mostly apply to the key infrastructure that is supplied, as well as metrics such as power, temperature and the availability of the network. Most data centers give you a 100% uptime guarantee. The purpose of the remote hands S.L.A. is to identify the client’s expectations and service performance levels that will be provided, and offers remedies if the S.L.A. agreements aren’t met.

What needs to be included in a Service Level Agreement?


A good S.L.A. should contain the following aspects:

⬤  The type of services that are provided
⬤  The performance level of the supplied services, including reliability and availability
⬤  The monitoring and reporting process
⬤  How to handle in case of issues with the service
⬤  Response and resolution time of issues
⬤  Consequences if the service provider does not meet its commitment


If an S.L.A. lacks one or more of the points above, we strongly advise you to talk to your provider about it.
